Tango is an art

It's the character in each of us, it's the 'ensemble' of life, reflecting all it's idiosyncrasy. Tango is not a fashion, but an addiction, a drug, and as such, the more you are exposed to it, the more you become an addict.

About

Mirjana is a Serbian tango dancer. She discovered the capability of expressing herself, through dancing and music, at her early age. She danced Serbian traditional folklore dances, while her fortuitous encounter with tango in Belgrade, in 2006, was a turning point of the definite continuation of her development as a dancer, in an Argentinian way.

In collaboration with Miša Plavšić, the doyen of Serbia’s tango scene, with whom she began exploring this form of art, trained by Andrija Jovanović, dancing instructor in the Aster School of Dance and at seminars with top-notch instructors including Angel Coria, Demian Garcia, Aurora Lubiz, Jorge Firpo, Hugo Daniel, DNI Tango, Alejandra Mantinian, Gachi Fernandez, Natacha Poberaj in Buenos Aires, as well as with Ilias Oikonomou and Mauricio Castro in Hamburg, Mirjana has gained experience and enhanced her skills.

According to Mirjana, tango works as adrenalin, which is a product of the communication between the dancing partners, inexhaustible possibilities of improvisation and the concept of the dance, based on the natural flow of movements. Thus far, she has had more than 70 public appearances.
